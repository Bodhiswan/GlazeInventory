### [Wax Resist / Why It Works On Clay But Not Like Paint]
**Statement:** Wax resist works by blocking water from soaking into porous clay or bisque, so water-based slips, underglazes, and glazes cannot wet and bond to the waxed area; this is why it is most effective on absorbent surfaces and much less useful once a surface is already vitrified or sealed.
**Source:** Ceramic Arts Network, "A Cooler Alternative to Hot Wax Resist" (Roberta Penn), https://ceramicartsnetwork.org/daily/article/A-Cooler-Alternative-to-Hot-Wax-Resist ; Mayco, "AC-302 Wax Resist", https://www.maycocolors.com/product/ac-302-wax-resist/
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This is a mechanics-of-absorption issue, not a chemical reaction. It matters most for greenware, bisque, and raw underglaze workflows.

### [Wax Resist / Hot Wax, Cold Emulsion, And Latex Behave Differently]
**Statement:** Hot wax methods are valued for a hard, crisp resist line but require heating equipment and burn-risk management; water-based wax emulsions are easier for routine brushing, sponging, or foot-waxing; latex resist forms a peelable mask and is useful when the resist must be physically removed before firing rather than burned away.
**Source:** Ceramic Arts Network, "A Cooler Alternative to Hot Wax Resist" (Roberta Penn), https://ceramicartsnetwork.org/daily/article/A-Cooler-Alternative-to-Hot-Wax-Resist ; Ceramic Arts Network, "Resistance Is Not Futile! 7 Wax Resist Techniques for Pottery Decoration" (Courtney Long), https://ceramicartsnetwork.org/daily/article/Resistance-Is-Not-Futile-7-Wax-Resist-Techniques-for-Pottery-Decoration
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** Cold wax is better aligned with Glaze Library's likely audience because it suits brush-on and classroom/studio workflows better than hot paraffin systems.

### [Wax Resist / Water-Based Wax Over Raw Glaze Can Lift The Glaze]
**Statement:** Applying liquid wax resist over an unfired glaze layer can peel or tear that glaze unless the glaze contains enough gum or binder, because the wax film can grab the weak, dusty surface as it dries or when it is removed.
**Source:** AMACO, "Wax Resist", https://shop.amaco.com/glazes-underglazes/underglaze/glaze-additives-and-aids/wax-resist/
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This is especially relevant when people try to use wax as a masking tool on commercial brush-on glazes that are already powdery at the surface. It is safer on bare bisque or over well-bonded underglaze layers.

### [Wax Resist / Residue Can Trigger Pinholes Under Clear Glaze]
**Statement:** Residual wax can create micro-bubbles and pinholes under clear glaze, especially in low-fire transparent-over-underglaze systems, because the wax burns out and disturbs an otherwise fragile, clear glass surface.
**Source:** Digitalfire picture note, "Wax resist inducing surface pinholes on a low fire transparent over an underglaze", https://digitalfire.com/picture/2847
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This is a strong caution for decorative workflows using clear glaze over underglaze. Cleaning residue and avoiding unnecessary wax in decorated areas matters more than many hobby tutorials imply.

### [Tape And Shellac Resist / Hard Masks Usually Give Cleaner Edges Than Brushed Wax]
**Statement:** Hard masking methods such as tape or shellac generally give crisper, more geometric edges than brushed wax resist because they reduce brush drag, pinholing from wax residue, and uneven film thickness at the boundary.
**Source:** Ceramic Arts Network, "Resistance Is Not Futile! 7 Wax Resist Techniques for Pottery Decoration" (Courtney Long), https://ceramicartsnetwork.org/daily/article/Resistance-Is-Not-Futile-7-Wax-Resist-Techniques-for-Pottery-Decoration ; AMACO, "Water Etching with Velvet Underglaze", https://amaco.com/resources/tutorials/water-etching-with-velvet-underglaze
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** Tape is most useful for linear graphic work; shellac is useful when a wet sponge will later remove the unprotected underglaze from bone-dry ware.

### [Shellac Water-Etching / Bone-Dry Timing And Cleanup Are Part Of The Process]
**Statement:** In shellac water-etching, AMACO specifies applying underglaze to bone-dry ware, painting shellac over the areas to keep, then using a wet sponge to remove unprotected underglaze; the bisque must then be cleaned thoroughly before glazing to remove shellac residue.
**Source:** AMACO, "Water Etching with Velvet Underglaze", https://amaco.com/resources/tutorials/water-etching-with-velvet-underglaze
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** The cleaning step is important because resist residue left into the glaze firing can interfere with glaze surface quality.

### [Sgraffito / Best Timing Is Leather Hard Clay With Dry-To-Touch Underglaze]
**Statement:** For underglaze sgraffito, AMACO's guidance is to let the underglaze become dry to the touch while the clay remains leather hard, then carve; this timing keeps the clay firm enough to hold a line while still soft enough to cut cleanly without excessive chipping.
**Source:** AMACO, "Enhancing Surface with Velvet Underglazes", https://amaco.com/resources/tutorials/enhancing-surface-with-velvet-underglazes ; AMACO, "Amphora Project: Heroes and Handbuilding", https://amaco.com/resources/lesson-plans/amphora-project-heroes-and-handbuilding
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** If the underglaze is still wet, the line smears; if the clay is too dry, the tool tends to chatter and shed crumbs rather than cut smoothly.

### [Sgraffito / Tool Choice Changes Line Quality]
**Statement:** Needle tools, bamboo skewers, pencils, and purpose-made sgraffito tools produce different line widths and debris profiles; sharper point tools cut cleaner, narrower lines, while broader tools expose more clay and create more crumb cleanup before glazing.
**Source:** AMACO, "Enhancing Surface with Velvet Underglazes", https://amaco.com/resources/tutorials/enhancing-surface-with-velvet-underglazes ; AMACO, "Amphora Project: Heroes and Handbuilding", https://amaco.com/resources/lesson-plans/amphora-project-heroes-and-handbuilding
**Confidence:** Medium
**Verification:** Direct web source reading plus synthesis from the listed process descriptions
**Notes:** The mechanism is physical, not chemical: tool geometry and moisture state determine whether the edge compresses, cuts, or tears.

### [Mishima / Inlay Needs A Shrinkage-Compatible Fill And A Clean Scrape Stage]
**Statement:** Mishima-style inlay works by forcing contrasting slip or underglaze into incised lines, then scraping the surrounding surface clean; the fill material must dry and fire compatibly with the clay body or the inlay can crack, flake, or blur under later glaze.
**Source:** AMACO, "More Colorful Mishima", https://amaco.com/resources/tutorials/more-colorful-mishima ; Digitalfire, "Are Your Glazes Food Safe or are They Leachable?" (engobe/body fit discussion), https://digitalfire.com/article/12
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** Digitalfire's fit warning is broader than Mishima specifically, but it applies directly: slips and engobes must fit the body in drying, shrinkage, and thermal behavior.

### [Mishima / Wax Between Underglaze Layers Improves Separation]
**Statement:** AMACO's Mishima workflow uses wax resist between colored underglaze applications so later colors can be removed from unwanted areas without contaminating earlier inlay zones, effectively turning resist into a registration and cleanup aid rather than only a glaze-blocking tool.
**Source:** AMACO, "More Colorful Mishima", https://amaco.com/resources/tutorials/more-colorful-mishima
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This is especially relevant for decorative, multi-color commercial underglaze workflows where the goal is clean graphic separation before the clear glaze stage.

### [Slip Trailing / Viscosity Must Be High Enough To Hold A Raised Line]
**Statement:** Slip trailing requires a thicker, more gelled material than a normal brushing coat, because the line has to hold its height after extrusion; if the mix is too fluid it slumps flat before drying and loses relief.
**Source:** Ceramic Arts Network, "Resistance Is Not Futile! 7 Wax Resist Techniques for Pottery Decoration" (Courtney Long), https://ceramicartsnetwork.org/daily/article/Resistance-Is-Not-Futile-7-Wax-Resist-Techniques-for-Pottery-Decoration ; AMACO, "Slip Decorating Mug with White Slip", https://amaco.com/resources/tutorials/slip-decorating-mug-with-white-slip
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** Raised decoration will also soften more at higher temperatures, so low-fire and underglaze-trailing effects usually stay sharper than fluid cone-6 or cone-10 glaze-trailing effects.

### [Underglaze Under Clear / Clear Glaze Usually Deepens Color Rather Than Leaving It Unchanged]
**Statement:** Commercial underglazes often fire with a matte or velvety surface when left bare, but intensify in color under a clear glaze because the clear layer changes how light enters, reflects from, and exits the colored layer.
**Source:** AMACO, "(V) Velvet Underglaze", https://shop.amaco.com/glazes-underglazes/underglazes/v-velvet-underglaze/ ; Mayco, "Fundamentals Underglazes", https://www.maycocolors.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/2020-Fundamentals_web-1.pdf
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This optical effect is one reason clear thickness matters: too much clear can also magnify defects, blur edges, or mute surface texture.

### [Underglaze Under Clear / No Single Clear Works For Every Color System]
**Statement:** Digitalfire documents that some underglaze colors bleed, cloud, or fail under otherwise good clear glazes, and notes that chrome-tin pinks in particular may require a clear with low calcium, no zinc, and controlled boron; in practice, not every transparent glaze works with every underglaze palette.
**Source:** Digitalfire, "Underglaze", https://www.digitalfire.com/glossary/underglaze/1000 ; AMACO, "Water Etching with Velvet Underglaze" (HF-9 Zinc Free Clear example), https://amaco.com/resources/tutorials/water-etching-with-velvet-underglaze
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This is a chemistry-compatibility problem, not just an application-thickness problem. Zinc-free clears are especially common recommendations around sensitive pinks and reds.

### [Underglaze Under Clear / Co-Firing Can Need A Hold To Burn Out Gases Cleanly]
**Statement:** Mayco recommends a 15-minute hold for low-fire clear-over-underglaze firings when underglaze and clear are co-fired together, because extra soak time helps the underglaze burn out and reduces trapped-bubble defects in the clear.
**Source:** Mayco, "Fundamentals Underglazes", https://www.maycocolors.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/2020-Fundamentals_web-1.pdf
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This is most relevant in low-fire commercial systems. At other cone ranges, longer soaks can also increase color bleed if the clear is very fluid.

### [Majolica / Modern White Bases Often Use Zircon Rather Than Tin]
**Statement:** Traditional majolica relied on tin-opacified white glaze, but modern commercial and studio majolica systems often use zircon opacifiers because they are cheaper and more available, even though zircon and tin do not behave identically in melt, opacity tone, or glaze fit.
**Source:** Digitalfire, "Majolica", https://digitalfire.com/glossary/majolica ; Ceramic Arts Network, "Decorating Techniques and Troubleshooting Tips for Majolica", https://ceramicartsnetwork.org/daily/article/Decorating-Techniques-and-Troubleshooting-Tips-for-Majolica
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This matters because many contemporary "majolica" discussions are really about white opaque earthenware decoration in general, not strict historical tin-glaze chemistry.

### [Majolica / High Zircon White Bases Trade Opacity For More Defect Risk]
**Statement:** Digitalfire notes that modern majolica bases commonly contain about 12-15% zircon opacifier to achieve adequate whiteness, but higher zircon additions can raise viscosity, lower thermal expansion, and increase crawling, pinholing, blistering, or shivering risk.
**Source:** Digitalfire, "Majolica", https://digitalfire.com/glossary/majolica
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This is one reason majolica systems are unusually sensitive to body choice, bisque maturity, and glaze thickness.

### [Majolica Brushwork / Overglaze Color Must Stay Thin And Fluid]
**Statement:** Majolica decoration works best when colorants are brushed on as thin, watery overglaze decoration over a stable white base; thick, pasty decoration can crawl, bead, or sit awkwardly on the opaque ground instead of integrating during the glaze firing.
**Source:** Ceramic Arts Network, "Decorating Techniques and Troubleshooting Tips for Majolica", https://ceramicartsnetwork.org/daily/article/Decorating-Techniques-and-Troubleshooting-Tips-for-Majolica ; Ceramic Arts Network, "Majolica-Style Glazing and Surface Decoration" (Rhonda Willers), https://ceramicartsnetwork.org/daily/article/Majolica-Style-Glazing-and-Surface-Decoration
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This is one of the biggest differences between majolica decoration and ordinary brush-on commercial glaze layering. The white base is the glaze; the decoration behaves more like concentrated color in that glaze field.

### [Oxide And Stain Washes / Flux Determines Whether A Wash Stays Dusty Or Melts In]
**Statement:** Oxide or stain washes can be used either unfluxed for dry, recess-only emphasis or fluxed so they partially melt into the glaze surface; Ceramic Stains recommends approximately 85% stain to 15% frit for a stable overglaze stain mix and notes that some stains need as much as 50:50 stain-to-frit to mature properly.
**Source:** Ceramic Stains & Glazes, "How to Use Overglaze and Underglaze Ceramic Stains", https://ceramicstainsandglazes.com/how-to-use-overglaze-and-underglaze-ceramic-stains/ ; Ceramic Arts Network, "Majolica-Style Glazing and Surface Decoration" (Rhonda Willers), https://ceramicartsnetwork.org/daily/article/Majolica-Style-Glazing-and-Surface-Decoration
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** The key difference is melt behavior. Unfluxed washes emphasize texture and wipe-back contrast; fluxed washes become part of the glassy surface and usually look richer but are less forgiving.

### [Oxide Washes / Decorative Metal-Rich Surfaces Should Be Kept Off Food Contact Areas Unless Tested]
**Statement:** Decorative surfaces rich in metallic oxides or colorants should not be assumed food-safe; Digitalfire specifically warns that high copper and manganese surfaces are leaching risks and recommends proven liner glazes on food-contact interiors rather than relying on decorative exterior glaze chemistry.
**Source:** Digitalfire, "Are Your Glazes Food Safe or are They Leachable?", https://digitalfire.com/article/12
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This caution applies directly to oxide washes, manganese-rich patinas, copper-bearing decorations, and some dramatic low-fire decorative surfaces. Decorative success and chemical durability are not the same thing.

### [Overglaze And Luster / These Are Separate Low-Temperature Firings, Not Just Another Brush Coat]
**Statement:** Overglazes, metallics, and lusters are designed for an additional low-temperature firing after the glaze firing; Mayco's overglaze guide lists china paint around cone 018, metallics around cone 019-018, and mother-of-pearl and gold lusters around cone 020-018.
**Source:** Mayco, "Guide to Overglazes", https://www.maycocolors.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Overglazes-Guide.pdf
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This is a separate decorative system from underglaze-under-clear. It requires an already fired glaze surface and its own temperature window.

### [Overglaze And Luster / Underfiring And Overfiring Cause Different Failures]
**Statement:** In Mayco's overglaze guidance, underfired gold can appear dull and rub off while overfired gold turns pale; mother-of-pearl can become matte if overfired; and overfired china paint can blister or permanently stain the glaze surface.
**Source:** Mayco, "Guide to Overglazes", https://www.maycocolors.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Overglazes-Guide.pdf
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Direct web source reading
**Notes:** This makes witness cones and repeatable low-fire schedules unusually important for overglaze work. The decorative layer is thin and has very little thermal forgiveness.
