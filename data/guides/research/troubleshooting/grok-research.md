# Troubleshooting Research

### Crawling — Glaze Pulling Away into Beads, Bare Spots (Multiple Causes Ranked by Likelihood + Fixes + Caveats)
**Statement:** Primary cause (rank 1): Dust, oil, or contaminants on bisque preventing adhesion (high surface tension/poor wetting); fix: thorough damp-sponge cleaning before glazing. Rank 2: Excessive glaze shrinkage from high raw clay content; fix: calcine part of clay or source alumina elsewhere (e.g., less EPK). Rank 3: Thick application or slow drying causing cracks; fix: thinner coats, faster even drying. Other: high surface tension glazes.
**Source:** Mayco Ceramics 101 (p. 18 crawling from dirt/dust/oil; accessed via browse_page); digitalfire.com/trouble/crawling (detailed causes/fixes, accessed via web_search).
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Manufacturer PDF + digitalfire practitioner database.
**Notes:** Common in all cone ranges but worse in high-clay low-fire glazes; conflicting old sources blame only application vs. modern (composition).

### Pinholing / Bloating — Tiny Holes, Trapped Gas, Body Swelling (Multiple Causes Ranked by Likelihood + Fixes + Caveats)
**Statement:** Primary cause (rank 1): Underfired bisque trapping organics/gases that release in glaze fire; fix: fire bisque 1-2 cones hotter (e.g., cone 04 vs. 06 glaze). Rank 2: Fast firing or poor kiln venting trapping gases; fix: slower ramp, hold/soak at peak. Rank 3: Carbonates/sulfates in body or high-viscosity glaze trapping bubbles; fix: slower bisque, adjust glaze fluidity. Bloating from overfiring body.
**Source:** Mayco Ceramics 101 (p. 12: underfired bisque primary for pinholes; accessed via browse_page); academic paper summaries (e.g., gas from body per peer-reviewed sources via search).
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Manufacturer PDF accessed directly.
**Notes:** Low-fire more prone (Cone 06); high-fire stoneware less if properly bisqued; conflicting advice on soak times.

### Crazing — Fine Crack Network in Glaze Surface (Multiple Causes Ranked by Likelihood + Fixes + Caveats)
**Statement:** Primary cause: Thermal expansion mismatch (glaze COE higher than body, glaze shrinks more on cooling); fix: increase silica or decrease fluxes in glaze, or adjust body. Rank 2: Underfired glaze (too soft); fix: fire to exact cone. Rank 3: Thick glaze or rapid cooling.
**Source:** Mayco (underfired notes p. 13); IU handbook and digitalfire (COE mismatch primary); peer-reviewed papers on CTE (via web_search).
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Multiple PDFs and academic summaries.
**Notes:** Worse in low-fire; high-fire stoneware needs careful fit; conflicting: some add zinc for fit but risks other defects.

### Shivering — Glaze Flaking Off in Sharp Slivers (Opposite of Crazing) (Multiple Causes Ranked by Likelihood + Fixes + Caveats)
**Statement:** Primary cause: Glaze COE lower than body (glaze under compression, flakes under stress); fix: increase fluxes or decrease silica in glaze to raise COE. Rank 2: Overfired glaze becoming too fluid. Fix: lower firing temp.
**Source:** Digitalfire.com and standard texts (cross-referenced); Mayco indirect via fit notes.
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Practitioner/educational sources aligned with academic COE research.
**Notes:** Opposite of crazing; rare in commercial glazes but common in mismatched high-fire.

### Running / Dripping — Glaze Flowing Off Vertical Surfaces (Multiple Causes Ranked by Likelihood + Fixes + Caveats)
**Statement:** Primary cause: Overfired (too much flux/low alumina) or too-thin application/SG; fix: fire to correct cone, increase alumina, thicken to proper SG. Rank 2: High-flux glazes at cone edge.
**Source:** Mayco (glaze "soft/hard" movement p. 17); handbook viscosity notes.
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Manufacturer PDF.
**Notes:** Common in low-fire flowing glazes; use stilts or catch basins for high-fire.

### Color Inconsistency — Uneven Color, Contamination, Batch Variation (Multiple Causes Ranked by Likelihood + Fixes + Caveats)
**Statement:** Primary cause: Contamination (metal tools, previous batches) or uneven thickness/application; fix: dedicated tools, consistent SG/coats. Rank 2: Batch variation in commercial glazes or raw materials; fix: thorough mixing/sieving. Rank 3: Firing atmosphere/temp variation.
**Source:** Mayco (mixing warnings p. 17); general manufacturer SDS/notes.
**Confidence:** Medium
**Verification:** Manufacturer guidance.
**Notes:** Colorants sensitive in reduction vs. oxidation; cone-specific.

### Blistering — Large Burst Bubbles, Moonscape Surface (Multiple Causes Ranked by Likelihood + Fixes + Caveats)
**Statement:** Primary cause: Trapped gases from underfired bisque or body impurities; fix: hotter/slower bisque, vented firing. Rank 2: Overfiring or high surface tension.
**Source:** Mayco (p. 12 blistering link to bisque); academic defect papers.
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Manufacturer PDF.
**Notes:** Similar to pinholing but larger; low-fire more susceptible.

### Dry / Underfired Glaze — Rough, Matte Surface That Should Be Glossy (Multiple Causes Ranked by Likelihood + Fixes + Caveats)
**Statement:** Primary cause: Underfired (did not reach maturation cone); fix: verify pyrometer/cones, fire hotter. Rank 2: Too-thin application or low SG.
**Source:** Mayco (p. 13: underfired = cloudy/milky/lacking sheen; accessed via browse_page).
**Confidence:** High
**Verification:** Original manufacturer PDF.
**Notes:** Exact cone critical; varies by kiln type (electric vs. gas).
